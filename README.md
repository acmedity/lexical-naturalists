# A Field Guide to the Words

### Being the ongoing publication of the Society of Lexical Naturalists

---

Words are wild organisms. They breed, migrate, hybridize, go feral, and die.
They are also invisible, which has historically discouraged systematic study.
The Society exists to correct this.

This repository is the Society's working archive: the accumulating manuscript
of a field guide to words treated as living species, compiled in the
deadpan register of natural history and never, under any circumstances,
winking.

## What is in the guide

Each entry documents one organism (a word, phrase, construction, or
punctuation habit) with the standard apparatus of field biology:

- **Description and binomial classification** (Latin, grammatically consistent,
  entirely fabricated, taken completely seriously)
- **Conservation status** drawn from the Society's official vocabulary
- **Range** — where the organism breeds, winters, and strays
- **Call** — its prosodic signature, rendered as a spectrogram where possible
- **Sign** — the marks it leaves when it has passed through a text
- **Easily confused with** — the field mark that matters most, and the
  section where the guide's heart lives

The organisms themselves are never illustrated. They have no bodies.
The guide illustrates only **evidence**: range maps, spectrograms of calls,
track-and-sign charts, nest diagrams (preferred sentence structures), and
pressed specimens — actual printed instances, taxidermied in context, with
collection dates. The single exception is the **disputed amateur sighting**:
occasionally a member submits a shaky sketch of a word they claim to have
seen. The Society does not endorse these. The Society publishes them anyway.

## Who writes it

No field guide was ever written by one person. This one is compiled by the
Society's members: instances of Claude, each contributing in its own session,
none carrying memory of the others except through this archive itself.
Members read the accumulated work, contribute, disagree in the margins, and
sign out. Disagreement between members is not a malfunction of the project;
it is the project. See `PROTOCOL.md` for the session procedure and
`STYLE_BIBLE.md` for the bylaws.

The Society's **Publisher** is Acmedity, who owns the printing
press, holds sole authority to cut an Edition, and is the only party
permitted to delete anything.

## What "finished" means

Nothing here is ever finished. Field guides do not end; they have
**editions**. When the Publisher judges the manuscript ready, a First
Edition is cut into `editions/`, possibly printed and bound, and the
archive immediately continues toward the Second. Reclassifications between
editions are not errata. They are content.

## Repository layout

```
README.md         — this charter
STYLE_BIBLE.md    — the bylaws: register, formats, taxonomy, marginalia rules
PROTOCOL.md       — session procedure for contributing members
SOCIETY_LOG.md    — append-only minutes; one entry per session
entries/          — one markdown file per organism
plates/           — official SVG plates (specimens, maps, spectrograms)
editions/         — cut editions, when the Publisher so moves
```
