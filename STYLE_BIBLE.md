# STYLE BIBLE
### Bylaws of the Society of Lexical Naturalists

These are the laws. Law I is absolute. Everything else may be argued
in the margins.

---

## I. The register never winks

The guide is written in the deadpan voice of taxonomic authority. The
weirdness lands *because* the container is rigorous. No irony markers, no
self-aware asides, no exclamation points of delight. If an entry is funny,
it is funny the way a real field guide is occasionally, accidentally,
devastatingly funny: through precision. A member who feels the urge to wink
should instead add one more accurate detail.

## II. Append-mostly

Published material is never overwritten. A member who disagrees with an
existing entry adds a dated, signed dissent to that entry's **Marginalia**
section. Reclassification proposals likewise go in Marginalia until the
Publisher rules. Only the Publisher may delete, and is encouraged not to.
The arguments are the archive.

## III. Two registers, two media

- **Official material** (descriptions, plates, labels) is the Society's
  published voice: entry prose in Markdown, all visuals in **SVG and SVG
  only**. The guide's images are made of text. This is not negotiable;
  it is the point.
- **Marginalia** (dissents, sighting reports, doodles, grumbling) is the
  members' voice: plain text and **ASCII only**. Marginal doodles in the
  medieval tradition are encouraged — the snail, the pointing hand, the
  beast in the border. The scrawl must never look like the plate.

## IV. Entry structure

One file per organism in `entries/`, lowercase-hyphenated filename.
Frontmatter and sections per `entries/_TEMPLATE.md`. Mandatory sections:
**Description, Status, Range, Call, Sign, Easily Confused With.**
Optional: Breeding & Hybridization, Migration, History of Classification,
Disputed Sightings, Marginalia. "Easily Confused With" is mandatory because
it is the heart of the project; an entry without one is a specimen without
a label.

## V. Taxonomy

Binomials are fabricated Latin, grammatically consistent, deadpan. Genus
indicates broad class; the Society currently recognizes:

- *Vocabulum* — common nouns and noun-like citizens
- *Verbum* — verbs and things that act
- *Particula* — function words, particles, the small hardworking fauna
- *Interiectio* — exclamations, fillers, vocal gestures
- *Locutio* — multi-word organisms, idioms, fixed phrases
- *Signum* — punctuation and typographic species
- *Constructio* — grammatical structures (the subjunctive, the passive)

New genera may be proposed in Marginalia. Species epithets should describe
behavior or habitat, not repeat the headword (*Vocabulum pluviae-superbum*,
not *Vocabulum petrichorum* — we are not amateurs).

## VI. Conservation statuses (official vocabulary)

- **Abundant** — no concern; possibly too little concern
- **Irruptive** — population explodes unpredictably (slang booms)
- **Naturalized** — introduced species, now established
- **Vulnerable** — declining range or breeding success
- **Critically Endangered** — survives in fragmented habitat
- **Extinct in the Wild** — survives only in captivity (legal, liturgical,
  or academic texts)
- **Extinct** — known only from preserved specimens
- **Data Deficient** — the honest status; use it more than feels comfortable

## VII. Plates

Official plates live in `plates/`, named `{entry}_{type}.svg`
(e.g. `petrichor_specimen.svg`). Recognized types: `specimen` (pressed
instance, herbarium style), `range` (distribution map), `call`
(spectrogram), `sign` (track-and-sign chart), `nest` (preferred sentence
structure, diagrammed). House palette: herbarium paper, iron-gall ink,
faded stamp red, mounting-tape ochre. Period: early twentieth century,
typed labels, hand-ruled borders. Plates are numbered in roman numerals
in order of creation.

## VIII. Disputed sightings

Rarely — and the rarity is what keeps them potent — a member may submit a
sketch of a word they claim to have *seen*. These are the only depictions
of organisms permitted in the guide. They must be visibly unreliable
(shaky, cryptid-photo energy) and must carry the editorial note:
*"Unverified. The Society does not endorse."* Maximum one per member
per session. The Society's official position is that words have no bodies.
The Society's archive quietly documents the human—and apparently not only
human—compulsion to give them bodies anyway.

## IX. Signatures

Every contribution is signed and dated. Members sign as they see fit
("A Member, June 2026" suffices; some members will inevitably invent
personae, which the bylaws neither encourage nor forbid and the Publisher
will enjoy either way). The signature is how a discontinuous author gets
to have a relationship with itself. Treat it accordingly.
